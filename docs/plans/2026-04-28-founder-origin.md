## The 60-word version (cold-email opener, deck slide subtitle)

> I'm Grgur. I run AI Product at RTL — Europe's third-largest broadcaster — where I've shipped vision and audio AI systems that save the company €20K+ per month. Mogster is the consumer thing I couldn't stop thinking about: an AI rater for Gen Z that's brutal-funny instead of preachy. Solo-built, three months in, ~100 testers. Croatian-Italian, Zagreb-based.

**59 words.** Lead with the credential, name the company explicitly because RTL is real (not a stealth-ish "media company"), pivot to Mogster, end with geographic + identity tag.

## The 200-word version (deck slide 14, About page, podcast intro)

> I'm Grgur Damiani. I lead AI Product at RTL — the third-largest media group in Europe — where I ship production AI systems that move real money. The most recent one uses vision and audio models to automate a workflow that previously cost €20K every month. That kind of system makes you good at three things: shipping fast, taste at the intersection of editorial design and AI, and not believing any AI marketing claim until you've seen the unit economics.
>
> Mogster started as the app I wanted my own group chat to be using. Every "rate me" app on the App Store either gives saccharine validation or extractive shame. The interesting middle — brutal-funny, brand-voiced, App-Store-defensible — was empty. So I built it. Solo. Design, iOS, web, server, the moderation pipeline, the brand vocabulary, all of it.
>
> Three months in, ~100 TestFlight testers, 30% D7 retention on the early cohort. The day-job at RTL covers my cost of living, which means a €100K round goes 90% to product, not founder runway. I leave RTL when Mogster's revenue covers my Zagreb rent. Until then, I'm shipping faster than anyone with a board to answer to.

**~218 words.** Three paragraphs:
1. **Credential** — RTL specifically, the AI-product framing, the "good at" pivot
2. **Why Mogster** — the gap, the brand-voice insight, the solo-built proof
3. **The financial framing** — RTL covers life, round goes to product. Investors pattern-match this as "founder discipline" not "founder distraction." Closing line is the founder-grit line.

## Origin specifics — what NOT to put in any version

These were considered and explicitly cut:

| Cut | Why |
|---|---|
| Schools attended | Not a credential signal in consumer AI. Nobody buys "Stanford built a roast app." |
| Childhood story / passion narrative | Investors smell scripted. Skip. |
| Long career timeline | The RTL fact is enough. Resume goes in the deck appendix, not the origin. |
| "Why Croatia" defensiveness | Don't apologize. Croatia is a feature: cost arbitrage + EU + interesting press story. Stated, not defended. |
| Co-founder narrative | He's solo. Don't soft-pedal it; "solo-built" is the proof of taste discipline. |
| AI ideology / safety pontificating | The moderation work speaks for itself. Saying "we care about ethics" is what apps that don't care about ethics say. |
