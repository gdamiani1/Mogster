## Why this script works (annotated breakdown)

### 0:00–0:05 — The "App Store wants this" hook
The single biggest pre-launch fear in consumer AI 2026 is App Store rejection. Looksmaxxing apps have been pulled. Leading with "the App Store actually wants on its platform" tells investors *we already thought about it.* Implicitly references the moderation work without saying "we have moderation."

### 0:05–0:18 — Show the loop, don't describe it
Every consumer-AI demo is too long because founders explain their tech. Mogster's demo shows the loop: pic → lens → score. No mention of Gemini, no mention of safety filters, no mention of prompts. The product IS the proof.

### 0:18–0:30 — The card is the ad
This is the pitch's most important sentence. "Every share is an install ad" is the K-factor thesis in five words. Investors who track consumer AI know to look for this. If they don't ask about it, the slide already answered them.

### 0:30–0:42 — Network effects shipped
Leaderboards + battles aren't a roadmap item — they're shipped product. This separates Mogster from "AI tool with no retention plan." Battle being shipped "next week" is fine; the leaderboard is live, that's enough proof.

### 0:42–0:58 — Monetization without ads
"Sub-first" is the deck's slide-11 line. Pre-empts the "but how will you make money on a teen app where ads can't target?" question. €144K Y1 ARR is conservative-but-real, not a hockey stick.

### 0:58–1:14 — Retention numbers
30% D7 on a real cohort beats hand-waving. Including "three-month-old TestFlight cohort" is honest — not pretending it's launched. Investors who ask "is this real?" have their answer.

### 1:14–1:28 — The ask
"100K to ship public launch and prove K-factor" frames the round as proof-spending, not survival-spending. Investors hear: "this person knows what success looks like and what's left to prove." Includes the geographic detail (Croatia) because solo-Croatian-founder is a press story they'll remember.

### 1:28 — Tagline close
"Drop a pic. Get cooked." is the tagline. Ending on it reinforces the brand voice and gives them an earworm.

## Production notes

### Setup
- Screen-record on iPhone (TestFlight build) running on a real device, mirrored to Mac
- Record voice on a podcast-quality mic (Shure MV7 or similar). Phone audio is too tinny for a Loom.
- Record voice and screen separately, sync in post — Loom's "voice + screen" mode degrades both
- Or: use ScreenStudio (Mac) — it handles both with auto-zoom on taps

### Pacing
- Average shot length: ~7 seconds. No shot longer than 18s.
- Slam-cut between sections (matches the brand motion language)
- Hazard-yellow overlay flash between sections (3-frame, ~50ms) — visual "next chapter" beat

### Voice
- Conversational, not corporate. "I drop a selfie" not "the user uploads a photo."
- Light Croatian accent is fine — it's a proof of authenticity, not a problem to fix
- Slight smile in the voice. The product is fun. The pitch should sound like the founder is having fun.

### Don't include in the demo
- Logos of subprocessors (Supabase, Gemini, etc.) — too engineering-pitchy
- Code or technical architecture
- The team slide (it's a solo founder; redundant)
- Competitor comparison (waste of seconds)
- Long-term roadmap (mention Q3 launch, no further)
